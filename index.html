Homi Jehangir Bhabha

About the Legend

☛ In 1935 Homi Jehangir Bhabha obtained his Ph.D. from Cambridge University. His research was concerned with cosmic rays...

☛ He became the first scientist to enunciate the Cascade Theory of Showers now known as “Bhabha Scattering.” ...

☛ He played a key role in India’s nuclear program, founding the Tata Institute of Fundamental Research in 1945...

☛ Under his guidance, India had its first atomic research reactor in 1956 named “Apsara.” ...

☛ He was awarded the Padma Bhushan in 1954, and his contributions shaped India’s atomic and space programs.

☛ Bhabha tragically died on January 24, 1966, in an air crash on Mont Blanc, France.
